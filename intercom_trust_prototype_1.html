What if Intercom Fin routed on trust state,
not sentiment score?

Fin's current escalation logic detects frustration, repeated loops, and explicit requests for human help — all behavioural proxy signals. This prototype explores what changes when the system first asks why trust is low, before deciding what to do next.

Trust-Sensitive Escalation
Human-in-the-Loop Design
B2B SaaS · Customer Support AI
Intercom Fin · Redesign Concept
The gap
Fin escalates when it detects negative sentiment or complexity. Neither signal tells you why the user doesn't trust the answer — which determines what to do next.
The hypothesis
Brand skepticism and self-ambiguity look identical in sentiment scores but need opposite responses. Collapsing them into one escalation path gets one group wrong every time.
The design decision
Before escalating, the system inserts a trust classification gate — a single question that separates these two states and routes them differently.

Decision rationale
Three design decisions. Each one defended.
Every fork, judgment criterion, decision, and failure condition — documented.
DECISION 01
Trust classification gate before escalation
The fork
Route all low-trust signals to immediate human escalation (Fin's current logic) vs. insert a classification question first.
Criterion
Brand skepticism and self-ambiguity look identical in sentiment scores but need opposite responses. Collapsing them into one path gets one group wrong every time.
Decision
Insert classification gate. Brand skepticism → urgent AE. Self-ambiguity → SDR discovery brief (not AE, not content — a human whose job is to define the problem, not close it). Gave up: simpler logic, lower build cost, consistent agent workflow.
Context note
In a resource-constrained context (limited Sales/AM headcount), self-ambiguous users route to content-first with delayed outreach instead — preserving scarce human capacity for users who are already directionally clear. Same trust state, different system design, because the business model is different.
Fails when
15 consecutive low-trust cases show <10pp difference in response rate between the two paths → collapse back to single escalation.
DECISION 02
System declines to answer direction-unclear users
The fork
Fin attempts an answer using available knowledge vs. system refuses to answer and asks a clarifying question first.
Criterion
An answer to the wrong question increases confusion, not confidence. In high-cost B2B decisions, a misaligned answer is worse than no answer.
Decision
Decline to answer. Gave up: faster time-to-first-response, higher resolution rate metric in the short term.
Fails when
Users flagged as direction-unclear who receive a clarifying question have a lower satisfaction score than those who received a direct answer → revert.
DECISION 03
Hidden stakeholder detection triggers AE brief
The fork
Continue conversation as normal vs. detect "need to check with [title]" language and flag the deal with a stakeholder brief for the AE.
Criterion
Hidden stakeholders are the single most common reason B2B deals stall after a strong AI-assisted discovery. If the system can detect the signal, not flagging it is a product failure.
Decision
Flag and brief AE. Gave up: uninterrupted Fin conversation flow; risk of false positives creating noise for AEs.
Fails when
AE brief false positive rate >30% (AEs report irrelevant flags) → tighten detection threshold before re-enabling.
CORE BELIEF
"AI choosing not to answer is a trust signal — not a system failure."
Fin's bet
AI coverage maximisation — human is the fallback when AI can't resolve.
This bet
Trust state determines escalation — not problem complexity. An AI answer to a low-trust user in a high-stakes B2B decision makes things worse, not better.
Validated by
Decision Confidence System beta — 80% lead-to-consultation conversion using trust-state routing (n=5, invited sample).

Impact chain — if the design hypothesis holds
Impact 01
Fewer misrouted escalations
Problem
Fin currently routes all low-trust signals to human agents with no context about why trust is low. Agents arrive to find users who aren't ready to be sold to — wasting handle time and triggering avoidance.
Trust Layer changes
Classification gate separates brand skeptics (need immediate human) from self-ambiguous users (need problem definition first). Each group receives a contextually appropriate handoff — not a generic escalation.
Metric that moves
AE/SDR utilisation rate ↑
Cost-per-qualified-conversation ↓
Supporting: 73% of buyers avoid suppliers with irrelevant outreach (Gartner 2024) — misrouted escalations are not neutral, they are actively harmful.
Impact 02
Higher pipeline conversion at early stage
Problem
Self-ambiguous users — those who haven't yet defined their problem — are being met with product information before they're ready. They disengage. The deal dies before it enters the pipeline.
Trust Layer changes
Routes self-ambiguous users to an SDR whose job is problem definition, not deal closing. This creates a lower-pressure first human touchpoint at the stage when the buyer most needs it.
Metric that moves
Early-stage pipeline entry rate ↑
7-day demo/discovery booking rate ↑
Supporting: Buyers are 1.8× more likely to close a high-quality deal with digital + human hybrid vs digital alone (Gartner). The Trust Layer is the mechanism that creates the right hybrid at the right moment.
Impact 03
Brand trust at first contact
Problem
When escalations happen without context, agents repeat what Fin already said, or pitch before the user is ready. This is the moment most likely to generate the "overwhelmed and frustrated" response Gartner documented.
Trust Layer changes
Every escalation carries a trust-type brief. The agent or SDR knows whether to lead with empathy and evidence (brand skeptic) or curiosity and problem mapping (self-ambiguous). First contact quality improves.
Metric that moves
First-contact CSAT ↑
Time-to-trust ↓
Trial-to-paid conversion ↑
Supporting: Buyers are 32pp more likely to feel confident in a purchase decision after speaking with a rep vs AI alone (Gartner 2025). The brief ensures the human shows up ready to build that confidence.
The tension this design resolves
Gartner's paradox
67% of B2B buyers prefer rep-free — but self-service buyers are 1.65× more likely to regret their purchase. Buyers want to avoid reps, but they need them at specific moments.
What Fin does today
Routes to human based on frustration signals — which means the human appears when the relationship is already damaged, not when trust is still being formed.
What the Trust Layer does
Routes based on trust state — which means the human appears at the moment the buyer needs them most, in the form that matches why trust is low. That's the 1.8× multiplier, applied correctly.
If I were proposing this internally
Validation Plan
How I would design the experiment to prove — or disprove — the Classification Gate hypothesis at Intercom's scale.
Hypothesis
The Classification Gate improves outcomes for low-trust users without degrading overall resolution metrics.
Specifically: routing self-ambiguous users to content-first (nurture) rather than immediate human escalation will increase their 7-day consultation booking rate, without increasing cost-per-resolution for the overall Fin population.
Experiment Design
A/B test on low-trust signal population only
Control: Fin's current logic — all low-trust signals escalate to human agent immediately.

Treatment: Classification Gate inserted — one question routes to brand-skeptic (urgent) or self-ambiguous (nurture-first, 48h delayed outreach).

Scope: B2B accounts on Sales/Service Hub trial or evaluation stage only — where trust state has the highest impact on conversion.
Metrics
Primary 7-day consultation / demo booking rate for low-trust users
Guardrail Overall CSAT score; cost-per-resolution; human agent handle time — must not degrade vs. control
Classification accuracy % of classified users whose subsequent behaviour matches predicted type (brand-skeptic vs. self-ambiguous)
Learning metric Response rate delta between nurture path and urgent path — the core untested assumption
Sample Size & Success Criteria
Assuming Intercom processes ~1M conversations/day, with low-trust signal rate of ~3% in B2B evaluation context = ~30,000 low-trust conversations/day.

To detect a 10pp lift in booking rate (25% → 35%) with 80% power at p=0.05: ~800 users per arm — achievable in under 24 hours at scale.
Ship if:
✓ Treatment booking rate ≥ control + 8pp (accounting for MDE uncertainty)
✓ Classification gate accuracy ≥ 75%
✓ Overall CSAT does not drop more than 0.1 points
✓ AE false-positive brief rate < 25%
If the test fails
What a negative result would mean — and what I'd do next.
If classification accuracy < 75%
The gate question itself is ambiguous. Rewrite the classification question. Consider implicit signals (CRM tenure, past conversation count) instead of explicit self-report.
If nurture and urgent show < 5pp delta
The self-ambiguity / brand-skeptic distinction doesn't matter at this scale. Collapse back to single escalation path. The classification overhead isn't worth it.
If CSAT drops in treatment
The gate adds friction that outweighs any routing benefit. Kill the gate entirely. Return to direct escalation and look for trust signals earlier in the conversation — before the user reaches a frustration state.

What the original prototype got wrong
Three decisions I made in the Lilai Ireland prototype that data and testing forced me to revise.
Original assumption
Nurture-first would meaningfully outperform immediate escalation
What the data showed
Sensitivity simulation: revenue delta between paths was ~NT$197K per 1,000 users under optimistic assumptions (nurture 20pp better). Too narrow to justify automation infrastructure before empirical validation.
Decision changed to
Manual test first. 10–15 cases. Measure real response rate delta before building anything automated.
Original assumption
Declarative copy was a neutral description
What stress test showed
"A consultant will reach out" is a pre-checked opt-in. For a user still comparing options, this signals "you're already a lead" before they've chosen to be one. Trust leakage at the moment it matters most.
Decision changed to
Opt-in checkbox, default unchecked. Users who don't check it still receive their full diagnostic report. Outreach is earned, not assumed.
Original assumption
All low-trust signals were the same problem
What beta testing showed
Two structurally different causes — brand skepticism and self-ambiguity — look identical in sentiment scores but need opposite responses. One needs human urgency; the other needs space.
Decision changed to
Classification gate before any routing. One question, two paths, two entirely different follow-up approaches — and a testable hypothesis about which works better.
